# Plan: Define /undo consistency with tool_results and partial-completion artifacts

Source: `requires/20260626_03_require.md`

---

## Goal

Define and implement a clear policy for whether `/undo` removes, retains, or marks-as-obsolete the `tool_results` rows and diagnostic artifacts belonging to the undone turn, then enforce the policy consistently in `/tool list` and `/tool show`.

---

## Scope

**In-Scope**
- Decide the undo artifact policy (recommended: mark as obsolete / soft-delete, not hard-delete, to preserve audit trail)
- Add `obsolete` flag or `undone_at` timestamp to `tool_results` table
- Update `undo_last_turn()` / `UndoService` to mark affected tool_results rows
- Update `/tool list` to hide obsolete rows by default (with `--all` flag to show)
- Update `/tool show` to indicate when a result belongs to an undone turn
- Add regression tests: "undo turn → /tool list does not show undone results"

**Out-of-Scope**
- Full redesign of `tool_results` storage model
- Full redesign of `/tool` commands beyond obsolete-awareness
- Handling partial-completion artifacts beyond marking them in `session_diagnostics`

---

## Assumptions

1. `tool_results` rows are linked to a turn by `(session_id, tool_call_id)` or similar — need to confirm FK path.
2. The preferred policy is soft-delete (mark obsolete) rather than hard-delete, to preserve audit trail.
3. `undo_last_turn()` already knows the range of deleted message_ids; tool_results rows for those messages should be marked obsolete.
4. Partial-completion artifacts (in `session_diagnostics`) can be left as-is on undo — they are diagnostic-only and do not appear in `/tool list/show`.
