## Title
[Medium] Define and implement workflow resume behavior after approval

## Priority
Medium

## Labels
workflow, approval, ux, state-machine

## Dependencies
H-01 is independent.
This issue may depend on workflow design decisions.

## Background
Workflow approval records are persisted and can be resolved via `/approve` or `/reject`. Startup recovery also restores pending approval state.

However, after `/approve`, the command currently resolves the approval record but does not clearly resume the suspended workflow task and proceed to the `verify` stage.

## Scope
Update:
- `agent/commands/cmd_workflow.py`
- `agent/workflow/state_store.py`
- `agent/workflow/workflow_engine.py`
- Workflow tests

## Required Changes
- Decide workflow resume behavior:
  - auto-resume immediately after `/approve`, or
  - require an explicit resume command, or
  - resume on the next workflow turn.
- If auto-resume is selected:
  - Add a `StateStore.get_task_by_id()` method.
  - Reconstruct the pending `TaskRecord`.
  - Re-run the workflow engine.
  - Ensure processed stages are skipped via idempotency.
- Define `/reject` behavior:
  - immediately mark the task as `halted`, or
  - let engine resume and halt at approval gate.

## Tests to Add
- `test_approve_resumes_verify_stage`
- `test_reject_marks_or_halts_task`
- `test_resume_does_not_rerun_plan_or_execute`
- `test_startup_recovered_approval_can_resume`

## Acceptance Criteria
- Approved workflow tasks can proceed after approval.
- Rejected workflow tasks halt cleanly.
- Plan and execute stages are not duplicated.
- User-facing behavior is documented.
